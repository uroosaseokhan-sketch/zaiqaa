From Farm to Dabba

Ground fresh. Every single batch.

No factory line, no six-month-old stock. Here's exactly what happens between a whole spice and the pack that reaches your kitchen.

01

Sourcing

We handpick whole red chilies, turmeric root, coriander seed and whole biryani spices from trusted growers — chosen for colour, oil content and aroma, not just yield.

02

Cleaning & Drying

Every batch is sorted by hand to remove stems, stones and dust, then sun-dried to the exact moisture level that keeps flavour locked in without inviting mould.

03

Stone-Grinding

We grind in small batches at low speed, keeping the temperature down so the natural oils that carry flavour and aroma don't cook off in the process.

04

Hand-Packing

Each 200g pack is sealed the same day it's ground — no warehouse time, no shelf sitting — so what reaches you is as fresh as what left the stone.

Why It Matters

Freshly ground isn't a slogan here — it's a timeline.

Most spice on a supermarket shelf was ground weeks, sometimes months, before it got there. Volatile oils — the part that actually carries flavour and aroma — start fading the moment a spice is milled. Grinding to order is the only real way around that.

Zaiqa never keeps ground stock sitting around. Every pack you receive was whole spice within days, not months, of reaching you.

  • Small batches, ground to order — not stockpiled
  • Low-heat grinding preserves natural oils & aroma
  • Nothing added — no anti-caking agents, no colour, no filler
